For further analysis, we chose to study only products that can be produced in France. “Exotic” products like bananas or coconut are not taken in account. We assumed that these products are constantly imported all year, and the justification of this importation does not change according to the season in France, as they can never be produced in France. Therefore, our tools are not representative of total food importation in France.

Focus on cereals
Among the top imported products are a lot of cereals like soy, colza seed and wheat. Looking at the importance of cereals in importation, we figured that a lot of these cereales might not be imported for human consumption, but mostly for livestock: In France, there are more than 19 millions cows, 25 million pigs and 48 million fowl that have to be fed in addition to the human population. Therefore, most imported products are aimed to produce meat in France.
Import vs Export
Most products are produced in France: France is the first agricultural power in Europe [http://www.lafranceagricole.fr/actualites/eurostat-la-france-premiere-puissance-agricole-de-lue-1,0,624436187.html], and is 8th in the world. It is also a major exporter of agricultural products. Imported products is therefore only a small proportion (7%) of products available in France. However, we can observe that the proportion of imported products rises in winter: This is due to the fact that less products are able to grow in France during winter. The small proportion of imported products in December is related to the harvest of most cereals during this month, as indicated by our source for seasonal products: Greenpeace.fr[https://www.greenpeace.fr/guetteur/calendrier].

Where are these products coming from ?

Top countries
Spain is by far the biggest importator to France. The bordering countries as Spain, Belgium, Germany and Italy are in the top 10 of importing countries, and the cost for transportation is therefore reduced. Moreover, this results is also explained by European legislation promoting agricultural trade between countries. One may wonder whether the types products imported from Spain are also produced in France. We may also wonder if we import products from Spain even though it is their season in France. To what extent is it necessary to import those products from Spain? Is the production in France not enough?
